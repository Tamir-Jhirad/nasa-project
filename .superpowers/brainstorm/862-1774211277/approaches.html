3 Approaches for the Satellites Dashboard

Each differs in scope and analytics depth. Pick the one that feels right.

A

Globe-First — Minimal

The 3D globe is the entire experience. KPI cards at the top (total tracked, countries, LEO count), sidebar with group filters + search, globe in the center with live orbit animation when satellites are selected. No charts, no table.

Pros

  • Fast to build
  • The globe is stunning on its own
  • Low data volume — no table rendering

Cons

  • Less analytical depth
  • No breakdown of the dataset
B

Full Analytics — Mirrors the Asteroid Dashboard

KPI cards + globe + 3–4 charts (orbit class distribution donut, launch year timeline bar chart, country breakdown, object type pie) + a searchable/sortable satellite data table. Navigation tabs at the top switch between Asteroids and Satellites.

Pros

  • Rich, analyst-grade insights
  • Consistent with asteroid page pattern
  • Tells the space congestion story

Cons

  • More components to build
  • Table with 8k rows needs virtualization
C

Globe + Smart Analytics — Recommended ★

KPI cards + globe (left, dominant) + 2 focused charts beside it (orbit class donut + launch timeline). Below: a constellation breakdown bar chart + condensed satellite list that reacts to globe selection. No sprawling table — the globe IS the data explorer.

Pros

  • Globe stays hero element
  • Charts add context without overwhelming
  • Selected satellite shows a detail panel
  • Feels distinct from the asteroid page

Cons

  • Slightly more layout work

Data analyst perspective — what the charts would reveal

Orbit class donut — ~70% of active sats are in LEO (below 2000 km), mostly Starlink. Shows just how crowded low orbit has become.

Launch year timeline — the Starlink explosion from 2019 onwards is the most dramatic trend in spaceflight history. A bar chart makes this visceral.

Country/operator breakdown — US dominates (~60%), followed by China, UK (Starlink), Russia. Shows geopolitical dimension of space.

Constellation summary — Starlink alone is 60%+ of all active sats. Putting that in context of GPS, ISS, weather sats creates a striking picture.
